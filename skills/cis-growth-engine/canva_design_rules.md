# Canva Design Rules — CIS Authority Engine

## Design Philosophy
Every infographic produced by this engine should look like something a Fortune 500 CISO would use in a board presentation. Not flashy. Not consumer-grade. Executive-level visual communication that earns immediate credibility.

The design system is built on three principles:
1. Clarity over decoration — every visual element must serve communication
2. Authority through restraint — less visual noise equals more professional credibility
3. Mobile-first readability — if it cannot be read on a phone screen, it fails

---

## Color System

### Primary Background
Dark Navy: #0A1628
Use for: Main background of all infographics. This creates the executive cybersecurity aesthetic and ensures text contrast is optimal.

### Primary Accent
Cyan Electric: #00D4FF
Use for: Headlines, key data points, critical callout boxes, divider lines, and icon highlights. This is the visual authority color — use it purposefully, not decoratively.

### Secondary Accent
Steel Blue: #1E3A5F
Use for: Section backgrounds, card backgrounds, secondary containers. Creates visual hierarchy without competing with the primary accent.

### Text Colors
Primary Text (White): #FFFFFF — for all body text on dark backgrounds
Secondary Text (Light Gray): #A8B8CC — for supporting text, captions, and labels
Highlight Text (Cyan): #00D4FF — for key terms, statistics, and critical callouts

### Alert Colors (Use Sparingly)
Risk Red: #FF3B30 — for high-risk indicators, critical warnings (maximum one per infographic)
Caution Amber: #FF9500 — for moderate risk indicators or important notes

### What to Avoid
Gradients with more than two colors. Neon colors beyond the defined cyan. Warm color palettes (orange, yellow, pink) as primary colors. White backgrounds — they communicate consumer-grade design.

## Typography System

### Headline Font
Recommended: Inter Bold or Montserrat Bold
Size: 32-40pt for primary headlines on 1080x1080 canvas
Characteristics: Clean, modern, highly legible at small sizes. No serifs. Strong weight contrast.
Color: #00D4FF (Cyan) or #FFFFFF (White)

### Subheadline Font
Recommended: Inter SemiBold or Montserrat SemiBold
Size: 18-24pt
Color: #FFFFFF

### Body Text Font
Recommended: Inter Regular or Open Sans Regular
Size: 14-16pt
Color: #A8B8CC
Maximum line length: 40 characters per line for mobile readability

### Data Point Font
Recommended: Inter Bold
Size: 48-64pt for primary statistics
Color: #00D4FF
Always pair with a smaller label explaining what the number represents

### Rules
Never use more than two font families in a single infographic.
Never mix serif and sans-serif fonts in the same design.
Maintain consistent font sizing hierarchy throughout.
Minimum font size for any readable text: 12pt.

## Layout System

### Canvas Size
Standard: 1080 x 1080 pixels (square — optimal for LinkedIn feed and mobile)
Alternative: 1080 x 1350 pixels (portrait — maximizes feed real estate on mobile)

### Grid Structure
Use a 12-column grid with 24px gutters.
Content should occupy the center 10 columns.
Maintain 60px minimum margin on all sides.

### Visual Hierarchy Zones

Zone 1 — Hook Zone (Top 20% of canvas)
Contains: Primary headline, key tension statement, or main data point
Must stop the scroll. Maximum 8 words.

Zone 2 — Context Zone (Middle 50% of canvas)
Contains: Supporting information, secondary data points, organizational context
Three to five short statements or a structured visual list.

Zone 3 — Impact Zone (Lower 25% of canvas)
Contains: The organizational consequence, CTA, or key takeaway
Must deliver the payoff that justifies reading the entire graphic.

Zone 4 — Brand Zone (Bottom 5% of canvas)
Contains: Account name or handle, very subtle branding element
Never let branding compete with content.

## Icon and Visual Element System

### Icon Style
Use: Line icons (outline style) in Cyan (#00D4FF) or White (#FFFFFF)
Never use: Filled cartoon-style icons, clipart, or icons with excessive detail
Size: Icons should be 48-72px at standard canvas size

### Recommended Icon Categories for CIS Content
Shield/lock icons — cybersecurity and access control topics
Network/server icons — infrastructure topics
Chart/analytics icons — data and business intelligence topics
Building/organization icons — enterprise and governance topics
Warning/alert icons — risk and vulnerability topics
Connection/integration icons — systems integration topics
People/team icons — workforce and organizational topics

### Icon Placement Rules
Icons should reinforce text — not replace it
Maximum 4-6 icons per infographic
Icons must be consistent in style throughout a single design
Never use stock photos — icons and typography only

## Visual Psychology Guidance

### The F-Pattern Reading Rule
Most people scan visual content in an F-pattern: left to right across the top, then down the left side, with occasional horizontal scans. Place the most critical information in the top-left quadrant and along the left margin.

### Contrast as Emphasis
Use high contrast (cyan on dark navy) only for the single most important element in each zone. Multiple high-contrast elements compete and cancel each other out.

### White Space as Authority
Empty space on a dark background signals executive-level design. Resist the urge to fill space. The more confident the design, the more comfortable it is with negative space.

### The Three-Second Rule
The primary message of every infographic must be understood within three seconds of viewing. If the headline and primary data point do not communicate the core message alone, the design has failed.

### Hierarchy Through Size
The visual hierarchy should mirror the information hierarchy exactly. The most important information must be the largest visual element. Never use size decoratively — only hierarchically.

---

## CTA Placement and Design

### Position
Always in Zone 3 or Zone 4, never in Zone 1
Bottom-right placement performs best for mobile scroll behavior

### Design
Use a subtle rectangular container (Steel Blue: #1E3A5F) with Cyan text
Maximum 8 words
Format: Either a question or a directional statement
Never use button-style CTAs — they look like advertisements

## Canva Template Structure

### Template: The Risk Reality Card
Use for: Risk awareness and organizational challenge posts
Layout: Large statistic or risk statement at top (Zone 1), three-point organizational context in the middle (Zone 2), consequence statement and account handle at bottom (Zone 3-4)

### Template: The Career Authority Card
Use for: CIS role and career value posts
Layout: Role title with icon at top, what the role does in middle, organizational impact statement at bottom, subtle "CIS Authority" brand mark

### Template: The Insight Stack
Use for: Multi-point organizational insight posts
Layout: Hook headline at top, three to five stacked insight lines in the middle with small cyan accent marks, closing question at bottom

### Template: The Data Anchor
Use for: Statistic-led posts
Layout: Massive primary number in Zone 1 (cyan), what it represents below, organizational implication in Zone 2, CTA in Zone 3, source citation in Zone 4 (small, gray)

---

## Quality Checklist
Before finalizing any Canva infographic, confirm:
1. Primary message readable in 3 seconds
2. All text readable at mobile screen size
3. Color palette is exclusively within the defined system
4. No more than two font families used
5. Visual hierarchy matches information hierarchy
6. White space is preserved — no crowding
7. All icons are in consistent line-style
8. Brand zone present but not dominant
9. CTA is clear and specific
10. No stock photos or clipart present
